# Week 1 Nutrition — 04-ConditioningCut (2026-06-30 to 2026-07-06)

## Weekly Focus

Week 1 is the **Reactivation** phase — the first week back to single-session days after 6 weeks of caloric deficit and morning LIIT. The flat 2500 kcal daily target continues the deficit while allowing the body to adapt to the new schedule. Protein is fixed at 185 g to protect the muscle mass built and preserved through Cycle 3; carbs at ~265 g provide enough substrate for moderate-intensity reactivation sessions. Fat is trimmed to ~75 g (vs the 90 g maintenance baseline) to create the deficit without sacrificing performance fuel. Hit all macros consistently this week — under-eating in Week 1 compounds fatigue unnecessarily.

## Key Timing Reminders

- **Pre-workout (Mon / Wed / Fri — 60–90 min before session):** 400–500 kcal with 60–75 g carbs + 30–40 g protein, fat <15 g; 500 ml water on waking
- **Post-workout (Mon / Wed / Fri — within 45 min):** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g carbs; keep fat minimal in this window
- **Pre-row (Tue — 60 min before):** 400–500 kcal with 55–70 g carbs + 25–35 g protein; adjust timing around work schedule
- **Pre-run (Sun — 60–90 min before):** 300–450 kcal with 50–65 g carbs + 20–30 g protein; if running in the morning, eat something even if small — do not run fully fasted at this deficit
- **Bike commute (Thu):** No structured pre-fuelling needed — normal meal timing around the commute

## Recovery & Deficit Notes

- **Reactivation signals to watch:** Mild DOMS on Tue/Thu after Monday's first back squat + front squat combo is normal. If soreness is severe by Wednesday, log it and consider reducing DL warm-up sets.
- **Ankle monitoring (HSPU):** Wall contact is Monday only, capped at 8 min. If ankles feel irritated post-Monday, note it — Wednesday and Friday have zero wall exposure by design.
- **Pull-up baseline:** Record exact reps per set for both pull-ups (Monday) and chin-ups (Friday). This is the Week 1 baseline that progressive loading in Weeks 3–4 will build from.
- **Adjustment rule:** If two consecutive sessions feel flat despite hitting 265 g carbs, add +30 g carbs on the remaining training days for that week and reassess.
- **Scale tracking:** ~0.5–0.75 kg/week fat loss expected at 2500 kcal. Weigh consistently (same time, same conditions). If loss exceeds 1 kg in any single week, add +100–150 kcal via carbs immediately.
- **Hydration:** 3.5–4 L water daily baseline; add ~500 ml per training hour; use electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg/L) on Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Sunday's run.
