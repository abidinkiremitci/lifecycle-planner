## Tuesday — Front Squat (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 air squats with 2 s pause at bottom
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 6 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 8 hip circles/side
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 slow arm circles/direction

### Strength (15–20 min)
- Front Squat 4×4 @ ~65–70% (target: 60–65 kg)
  - Tempo: 3-1-1 (3 s descent, 1 s pause, 1 s drive)
  - Cues: elbows high throughout, upright torso, push knees out, full depth
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- Accessory: 2 × 10 reverse lunges (bodyweight or light KB in goblet position) for quad & hip stability prep

### Metcon (AMRAP 20 min – target 18–20 min of work)
- 10 burpees — sole cardio modality
- 12 DB push press (2 × 15 kg — adjust so set breaks ≤ 2 times per round)
- 10 ring rows (feet on floor, body nearly horizontal — increase difficulty from Week 1)
- Target: 7–9 rounds; consistent pace on burpees, no sprinting early; DB push press should be unbroken or 8+4 max

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- HSPU progression (7 min): 4 × wall walk — aim for nose-to-wall hold 15–20 s (up from Week 1's 10–15 s); rest 45 s between sets
- Core (5 min): 3 rounds × (20 hollow rocks + 10 V-ups + 20 s plank hold)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side
- Box breathing 4-2-6 × 2 min

## Wednesday — Deadlift (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 glute bridges + 10 bodyweight Romanian deadlifts
  - 8 hip hinge with PVC (focus on neutral spine) + 6 scapular push-ups
  - 8 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 6 deep reverse lunges/side

### Strength (15–20 min)
- Deadlift 4×4 @ ~65–70% (target: 115–120 kg)
  - Cues: hips back before the pull, neutral spine, drive floor away, controlled 3 s descent
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- Accessory: 2 × 8 single-leg RDL (light DB or bodyweight) each side — control the hinge, no hip drop

### Metcon (AMRAP 20 min – target 18–20 min of work)

- 8 hang power cleans (50 kg — adjust so set is unbroken or 5+3 max)

## Thursday — Indoor Rowing

### Warm-up (5 min)
- 3 min easy row (rate 18 spm, long stroke, relaxed hands)
- 8 hip flexor rocks/side + 10 shoulder rolls + 10 thoracic twists seated on erg

### Main Row (30 min steady-state)
- **4,500 m** at easy aerobic pace
  - Target split: ≤2:45/500 m (RPE 5–6, conversational pace, nasal breathing preferred)
  - Stroke rate: 18–20 spm
  - Focus: long slow draw, full leg drive before any lean and arm pull — prioritize technique over split
  - If 4,500 m feels genuinely easy at ≤2:45, add 500 m; if breathing is laboured, cap at 4,000 m and note it
- Note: Row was skipped in Week 1 — this is your true baseline. Record your actual split so Week 3 has a reference.

### Cool-down (5 min)
- 2 min easy row (rate 16 spm)
- Wall calf stretch 2 × 30 s/side

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 2 is your Build phase — calories and carbs return to full baseline targets to match the increased training volume and load. After the conservative fuelling of Week 1, this week's nutrition goal is to properly support three functional fitness sessions, a rowing baseline, and a running session without any kind of deficit. Carb intake is the primary recovery lever this week: prioritise getting adequate carbs in the pre- and post-workout windows, especially around the Deadlift and Back Squat sessions which are your heaviest efforts. Protein stays fixed at 185 g throughout. Don't undershoot on functional fitness days — the load step-up demands the fuel.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Tue / Wed / Fri | ~3400 kcal | ~185 g | ~460 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance (Row / Run) | Thu / Sat | ~3200 kcal | ~185 g | ~410 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Mon / Sun | ~2700 kcal | ~185 g | ~290 g | ~90 g |

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Deadlift (Wednesday):** 400–600 kcal, 60–80 g carbs, 30–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before session; 500 ml water.
- **Post-Deadlift (Wednesday):** 40–50 g protein + 60–80 g fast carbs within 45 min; 500–750 ml water.
- **Pre-Back Squat (Friday):** Same structure as Wednesday — prioritise carb loading in the window before this session since it follows a rest/recovery day.
- **Pre-Row (Thursday):** Lighter pre-session meal acceptable (RPE 5–6 effort) — 300–400 kcal with moderate carbs; hydrate well (500 ml 60 min before + electrolytes).

### Recovery Notes
- Wednesday and Friday are back-to-back high-load days — ensure Thursday's endurance nutrition (~3200 kcal) also serves as inter-session recovery fuel, not just row prep. Don't skip carbs on Thursday.
- Saturday running follows Friday's back squat — legs will be under some residual fatigue. Prioritise Friday post-workout nutrition (carbs + protein) to support Saturday's session.
- Daily hydration baseline: 3.5–4 L water; add ~500 ml per hour of training. Include sodium (500–700 mg/L) on Thursday (rowing) and Saturday (running) sessions.
