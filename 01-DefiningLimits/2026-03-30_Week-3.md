## Monday — Front Squat (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row (rate 18 spm)
  - 10 air squats with 3 s pause at bottom
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 6 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 10 hip circles/side
  - 6 world's greatest stretch/side (lunge + thoracic rotation)
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 band pull-aparts

### Strength (15–20 min)
- Front Squat 4×3 @ ~75–80% (target: 65–70 kg)
  - Tempo: 3-1-X (3 s descent, 1 s pause, 1 s aggressive drive)
  - Cues: elbows high and proud throughout, upright torso under load, knees out, full depth, big breath brace before descent
  - Rest: 2.5 min between sets
- Accessory:
  - 2 × 8 goblet squat (20 kg KB) — pause 2 s at bottom, reinforcing front rack depth
  - 2 × 8 strict dips (rings or bars) — controlled 2 s descent

### Metcon (7 min ON / 2 min OFF / 7 min ON — VO₂max interval format)

**Format:** Two 7-minute AMRAP blocks separated by a 2 min active rest (walk, breathe, get ready). Score = total rounds + reps across both blocks. Same movement sequence each block.

**AMRAP 7 (×2, 2 min rest between):**
- 7 burpees — sole cardio modality
- 7 hang power snatches (40 kg — should feel challenging but cycle-able; adjust so set is unbroken)
- 7 strict pull-ups (scale to 5 reps or band-assisted to keep moving)

**Target:** 3–4 rounds per block (6–8 total). Intensity should be RPE 8–9 in the last 2 min of each block — this is meant to push your aerobic ceiling. The 2 min OFF is not a full recovery; you'll still be breathing hard at the next start. Keep burpees controlled, don't blow up on rep 1.

> **Why this format:** The 7/2/7 structure drives VO₂max adaptations — you sustain near-maximal aerobic output long enough (7 min) to stress central adaptations, briefly allow partial recovery, then repeat. Back-to-back shorter AMRAPs with limited rest consistently produce higher peak heart rates than a single long 20 min AMRAP.

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- HSPU progression (7 min):
  - 4 × wall walk to nose-to-wall hold 20–25 s — slow controlled descent; rest 40 s between sets
  - Goal: hold longer and with better body tension than Week 2
- Core (5 min):
  - 3 rounds × (20 hollow rocks + 10 V-ups + 15 s hollow hold at end)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 60 s/side (hip flexors stressed from squats + burpees)
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side (snatch shoulder demand)
- Wrist flexor & extensor stretch 45 s each (snatch demand)
- Box breathing 4-2-6 × 2 min (down-regulation after high-intensity block)

## Tuesday — Deadlift (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 glute bridges + 10 bodyweight Romanian deadlifts
  - 8 hip hinge with PVC (neutral spine focus) + 6 scapular push-ups
  - 8 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 6 deep reverse lunges/side
  - 8 KB Romanian deadlifts (light, 16 kg) — feel the hamstring load

### Strength (15–20 min)
- Deadlift 4×3 @ ~78–82% (target: 120–125 kg)
  - Cues: hips back before the pull, neutral spine throughout, drive floor away, squeeze glutes at lockout, controlled 3 s descent
  - Rest: 2.5–3 min between sets
- Accessory:
  - 2 × 8 single-leg RDL (per side, 16–20 kg KB) — control the hip hinge, no trunk rotation
  - 2 × 8 strict chin-ups (overhand → underhand grip, full range of motion)

### Metcon (EMOM 20 min — VO₂max-targeting EMOM)

**Format:** 5-movement EMOM, 20 rounds (4 rounds per movement, cycling through A→E). Each minute is its own effort — target 40–45 s of work, 15–20 s rest. If rest drops below 10 s, reduce reps by 1–2 next round.

| Minute | Movement | Reps / Load |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| A (min 1, 6, 11, 16) | Double unders — sole cardio modality | 35 reps (alt: 70 singles or 20 DU attempts + 30 singles) |
| B (min 2, 7, 12, 17) | KB swings — American full overhead | 15 reps @ 20 kg |
| C (min 3, 8, 13, 18) | Strict push-ups | 12 reps (scale: add 1–2 reps if easy; reduce to 10 if failing in later rounds) |
| D (min 4, 9, 14, 19) | Hang power cleans | 6 reps @ 60 kg — should feel like RPE 7 per set |
| E (min 5, 10, 15, 20) | Ring rows (horizontal, feet on floor) | 10 reps — 3 s controlled descent each |

**Target:** Consistent rest windows throughout. Minute A (DU) will challenge your jump rope under fatigue — this is where the VO₂max stimulus lives. Minute D (hang cleans at 60 kg post-DL) is the quality check — don't sacrifice bar path for speed.

> **Why this format:** A 20-min EMOM with a demanding cardio minute every 5th slot keeps overall intensity high while distributing the aerobic stress across the session. Pairing DU with barbell and gymnastics in rotating fashion mirrors competition demands and drives cardiac output through varied recruitment patterns.

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- HSPU progression (7 min):
  - 3 × 6–8 pike push-ups from an elevated surface (feet on box) — full ROM, head to floor and lock-out; rest 60 s
  - 1 × max rep wall-facing strict pike press attempt (note reps)
- Core (5 min):
  - 3 rounds × (12 dead bugs/side + 25 s side plank/side + 10 slow leg lowers)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Prone quad stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Lat band stretch 2 × 45 s/side (chin-up lat demand)
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 2 × 45 s/side (deadlift + EMOM hip drive)
- Supine 90/90 diaphragmatic breathing × 2 min

## Wednesday — Indoor Rowing (VO₂max Intervals)

### Warm-up (8 min)
- 3 min easy row (rate 18 spm, long slow stroke, relaxed — RPE 4)
- 10 thoracic twists seated on erg + 10 shoulder rolls + 8 hip flexor rocks/side
- 2 × 30 s build: start at 22 spm → push to 26 spm in the last 5 strokes; easy 30 s between

### Main Row — 5 × 1,500 m Intervals (VO₂max target)
- **5 rounds of:**
  - **1,500 m at target split ≤2:25/500 m (RPE 8–9)**
  - Stroke rate: 24–26 spm
  - Rest: **3 min easy paddle** between intervals (rate 16–18 spm — do NOT stop; keep moving)
- **Total work:** 7,500 m | **Total session time approx.:** 45–50 min
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Hold back — aim for 2:28–2:30. The first interval almost always feels deceptively easy.
  - Intervals 2–4: Settle into 2:24–2:26, your target zone.
  - Interval 5: Race — whatever you have left, push; target ≤2:22.
  - If any interval slips past 2:35, end the session there, note it, and we'll recalibrate.
- **Why this works:** 1,500 m intervals at ≤2:25 will put you at VO₂max effort (RPE 8–9) for long enough (~3:45 per piece) to drive central cardiac adaptation. The 3 min active rest allows enough partial recovery to repeat at quality. This directly targets your 10 km sub-50 min goal (requires a sustained ~2:30/500 m average).

### Cool-down (5 min)
- 2 min easy row (rate 16 spm, eyes closed, breath normal)
- Wall calf stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Seated hip opener (figure-four on erg) 30 s/side
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 30 s/side

## Thursday — Running (VO₂max Intervals)

### Warm-up (5 min)
- 3 min easy walk transitioning to light jog (~7:00 min/km)
- 10 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 10 ankle rolls/side + 10 calf raises
- 2 × 20 s strides at ~4:30 min/km pace — walk back recovery

### Main Run — 4 × 1,000 m Intervals (VO₂max target)
- **4 rounds of:**
  - **1,000 m at target pace 4:45–5:00 min/km (RPE 8–9)**
  - Rest: **90 s walk recovery** between intervals
- **Total work:** 4,000 m | **Total session time approx.:** 30–35 min
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Hold to 5:00/km — don't go out at 4:30. The restraint here defines the fourth interval.
  - Intervals 2–3: Aim for 4:50–5:00, settling into rhythm.
  - Interval 4: Push — target ≤4:45.
  - If any interval goes past 5:15/km, walk 2 min instead and note it.
  - Terrain: flat road/path preferred.
  - Always provide indoor alternative: 4 × 300 m on the rower at ≤1:55/500 m with 90 s rest.
- **Why this works:** 1,000 m repeats at 4:45–5:00 min/km stress your VO₂max system effectively. They're short enough to run faster than your 10 km target pace (~5:00/km) while accumulating enough volume (4 km of quality work) to drive aerobic ceiling adaptation. This maps directly onto your 10-km-sub-50-min goal.

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 3 is the Push phase and the highest-output week of the cycle before testing. On top of three functional fitness sessions, you have two dedicated VO₂max-interval sessions this week (rowing intervals Wednesday, running intervals Thursday) — the combination puts total weekly training stress at its peak. The primary nutrition lever this week is carbs: increase by 50–80 g on functional fitness days versus your Week 2 baseline to support the additional glycolytic demand of the interval formats. Do not under-fuel this week — high-intensity intervals deplete glycogen faster than aerobic base work, and inadequate carbs will blunt the training adaptation you're chasing. Protein stays fixed at 185 g. Fat stays at 90 g. Hydration and electrolytes are critical, especially around the rowing and running interval sessions where sweat losses and cardiac output are both elevated.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Mon / Tue / Fri | ~3570 kcal | ~185 g | ~505 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance (Row / Run) | Wed / Thu | ~3200 kcal | ~185 g | ~410 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Sat / Sun | ~2700 kcal | ~185 g | ~290 g | ~90 g |

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Front Squat + Intervals (Monday):** 400–600 kcal, 60–80 g carbs, 30–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. The 7/2/7 metcon format will spike HR fast — don't train fasted.
- **Post-Monday:** 40–50 g protein + 60–80 g fast carbs within 45 min; 500–750 ml water. You have a heavy deadlift day tomorrow — prioritise rapid glycogen refill tonight.
- **Pre-Deadlift + EMOM (Tuesday):** Same as Monday pre-session. The 20-min EMOM after heavy deadlifts is demanding — carb window matters.
- **Pre-Row Intervals (Wednesday):** 300–400 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 25–30 g protein — 60 min before; 500 ml water + electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg). 5 × 1,500 m at VO₂max effort will spike sweat rate significantly.
- **Post-Row:** 40–50 g protein + 60–80 g carbs within 45 min; 750 ml water. Running intervals tomorrow — glycogen replacement is essential tonight.
- **Pre-Run Intervals (Thursday):** 300–400 kcal, 50–60 g carbs — 60–90 min before. The 4 × 1,000 m repeats at RPE 8–9 are anaerobic adjacent — carbs are the fuel.
- **Pre-Back Squat + EMOM (Friday):** Same as Monday/Tuesday pre-session. Friday is the third functional fitness day — by now cumulative fatigue is real. Don't skip the pre-session carb window.

### Recovery Notes
- Monday → Tuesday is a back-to-back heavy functional fitness pair. Tuesday's post-session nutrition is the primary recovery lever for Wednesday's rowing intervals. Don't skip it.
- Wednesday → Thursday is a back-to-back high-intensity interval pair (row + run). This is the hardest two-day block of the cycle. Hydration and sleep between these two sessions matter as much as nutrition.
- Friday closes a high-volume week. Saturday and Sunday are active recovery nutrition days (~2700 kcal) — use them to genuinely rest: walk, sleep, stretch. Don't add extra training.
- Daily hydration baseline: 3.5–4 L water; add ~500 ml per hour of training. Include sodium (500–700 mg/L) and potassium on all interval sessions.
- **Sleep:** Target 8 h minimum this week. VO₂max adaptations from interval training occur primarily during sleep — don't sacrifice recovery time.
