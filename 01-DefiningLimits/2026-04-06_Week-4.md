## Monday — Front Squat 3–5 RM Test (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row (rate 18 spm)
  - 10 air squats with 3 s pause at bottom
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 6 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 10 hip circles/side
  - 6 world's greatest stretch/side (lunge + thoracic rotation)
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 band pull-aparts

### Strength — Front Squat 3–5 RM Test (20–25 min)
- **Warm-up ramp:**
  - 40 kg × 5 (feel the rack and front rack position)
  - 50 kg × 3
  - 57 kg × 2
  - 62 kg × 1
- **Build to a 3 RM:**
  - Set 1: 67 kg × 3 — opener, should feel comfortable
  - Set 2: 70 kg × 3 — assess bar speed at the top
  - Set 3: 72–75 kg × 3 — push if Set 2 was smooth; if fast, attempt a 5 RM back-off
  - Rest: 3 min minimum between all heavy sets
- Cues: elbows high and proud, upright torso, knees out, full depth, aggressive drive out of the hole
- **Record:** All attempts and final 3–5 RM — this becomes the next cycle's working max
- Accessory (skip if strength test ran long):
  - 2 × 8 goblet squat (20 kg KB) — pause 2 s at bottom

### Metcon — "Threshold" (For Time — Cycle Benchmark)

**Format:** For time. Named benchmark — repeat in future cycles to measure progress. Score = total time.

**21-15-9:**
- Thrusters @ 40 kg barbell
- Strict pull-ups (scale: use a band to ensure full range; do NOT kip)

**Target:** ≤12 min. Go unbroken on thrusters where possible. Scale band tension so you can complete at least 12–15 unbroken pull-ups in the first set. This is analogous to Fran but strictly pulled — your time today is the baseline for every future test of this benchmark.

> **Why this benchmark:** A descending ladder of thrusters + strict pull-ups is one of the clearest tests of combined upper-body power and aerobic capacity. At 40 kg it demands hip drive and shoulder endurance simultaneously. Recording this today closes the DefiningLimits cycle with a repeatable conditioning data point.

### Skill / Core (10 min)
- HSPU progression (6 min):
  - 3 × kick-up to wall → nose-to-wall hand balance hold — aim for 10+ s; rest 45 s between
  - 1 × max-effort freestanding kick-up attempt — try to hold 3–5 s; note result
- Core (4 min):
  - 3 rounds × (20 hollow rocks + 10 s hollow hold at end of each round)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 60 s/side (squat + thruster hip flexor demand)
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side
- Wrist flexor & extensor stretch 45 s each (thruster demand)
- Box breathing 4-2-6 × 2 min

- 2 × 30 s build: start at 22 spm → push to 26 spm in the last 5 strokes; easy 30 s between

### Main Row — 5 × 1,500 m Intervals (VO₂max target)
- **5 rounds of:**
  - **1,500 m at target split ≤2:25/500 m (RPE 8–9)**
  - Stroke rate: 24–26 spm
  - Rest: **3 min easy paddle** between intervals (rate 16–18 spm — do NOT stop; keep moving)
- **Total work:** 7,500 m | **Total session time approx.:** 45–50 min
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Hold back — aim for 2:28–2:30. The first interval almost always feels deceptively easy.
  - Intervals 2–4: Settle into 2:24–2:26, your target zone.
  - Interval 5: Race — whatever you have left, push; target ≤2:22.
  - If any interval slips past 2:35, end the session there, note it, and recalibrate.

### Alternative Main Row — 6 × 500 m Sprints (20–25 min total)

### Metcon — "Engine" (For Time — Cycle Benchmark)

**Format:** For time. Named benchmark — repeat in future cycles. Score = total time.

**3 rounds:**
- 12 cal row — sole cardio modality
- 21 American KB swings @ 24 kg
- 12 strict pull-ups (scale: band to complete ≥8 unbroken in round 1)

**Target:** ≤16 min. The row gets heart rate up fast going into the swings — don't sprint the first row. Swings at 24 kg should be unbroken rounds 1–2. Pull-ups breaking to 2 sets by round 3 is acceptable. Overall pace should feel like RPE 7–8 through round 2, then push on the final round.

> **Why this benchmark:** Row + KB swings + strict pull-ups across 3 rounds is a balanced aerobic-power test. The row demands cardiovascular output; the swings demand hip explosiveness; the pull-ups demand posterior chain and grip endurance under accumulated fatigue. This closes the cycle with a well-rounded conditioning data point.

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 4 is the Test week — the culmination of the DefiningLimits cycle. You are testing 3–5 RMs on all three major lifts and completing two VO₂max endurance sessions carried over from Week 3. This makes it a maximum-output week across five sessions. The primary nutrition lever is glycogen saturation: carb-load in the 24–36 hours before each max-effort session. Protein stays fixed at 185 g/day. Fat stays at 90 g. Do not undercut calories this week — maximal neural recruitment in 3RM attempts and sustained VO₂max output in interval sessions both require fully loaded glycogen. Sleep (8+ h) is equally important: strength adaptations and aerobic ceiling gains from the past three weeks consolidate during sleep, not during the session.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FF Test days (strength maxes) | Mon / Wed / Fri | ~3620 kcal | ~185 g | ~500 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance VO₂max sessions | Tue / Thu | ~3460 kcal | ~185 g | ~460 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Sat / Sun | ~2700 kcal | ~185 g | ~290 g | ~90 g |

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Front Squat Test (Monday):** 400–600 kcal, 60–80 g carbs, 30–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Carb-load Sunday lunch onwards to saturate glycogen before the 3RM attempt.
- **Post-Monday:** 40–50 g protein + 60–80 g fast carbs within 45 min; 500–750 ml water. Tuesday is a VO₂max row session — rapid glycogen refill tonight is critical.
- **Pre-Row Intervals (Tuesday):** 300–400 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 25–30 g protein — 60 min before; 500 ml water + electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg). 5 × 1,500 m at RPE 8–9 will spike sweat rate.
- **Post-Row:** 40–50 g protein + 60–80 g carbs within 45 min; 750 ml water. Deadlift test tomorrow — glycogen replacement tonight is essential.
- **Pre-Deadlift Test (Wednesday):** 400–600 kcal, 60–80 g carbs, 30–40 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Tuesday night nutrition is your carb-load window.
- **Pre-Run Intervals (Thursday):** 300–400 kcal, 50–60 g carbs — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. 4 × 1,000 m at RPE 8–9 are glycolytic — don't go in depleted.
- **Pre-Back Squat Test (Friday):** 400–600 kcal, 60–80 g carbs, 30–40 g protein — 60–90 min before. Thursday night nutrition is the carb-load window for Friday's final test.
- **Post-Friday:** Prioritise 40–50 g protein and moderate carbs (50–60 g); hydrate well. The weekend is genuine recovery — walk, stretch, sleep.

### Recovery Notes
- **Sunday (pre-week):** Target 8+ h sleep; begin carb-loading Sunday lunch (~430 g carbs) to saturate glycogen before Monday's 3RM. This is the biggest single nutritional lever for strength test performance.
- **Monday → Tuesday back-to-back (strength + VO₂max row):** Tuesday night nutrition is non-negotiable. Don't skip the post-session window.
- **Wednesday → Thursday back-to-back (deadlift test + run intervals):** This is the hardest two-day block of the cycle. Wednesday night sleep and nutrition take priority above everything else.
- **Daily hydration baseline:** 3.5–4 L water; add ~500 ml per hour of training. Include sodium (500–700 mg/L) and potassium on all interval sessions (Tue + Thu).
- **Weekend:** Sat/Sun are active recovery only. No added training. Walk, foam roll, sleep 8+ h. The adaptations from this four-week cycle consolidate during rest — protect it.
