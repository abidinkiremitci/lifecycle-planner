# Week 5 Nutrition — 03-FatBurning (2026-06-08 to 2026-06-14)

## Weekly Focus

Week 5 is the **Final Push** phase — the cycle's peak output week. Calories and carbs return to full deficit baseline across all training days, aligned with the increased training load. Strength loads step up significantly from W4's back-off (Front Squat +5 kg vs W3 / +15 kg vs W4, Deadlift +10 kg, Back Squat +10 kg), and metcons run 22–26 min with high-intensity cardio on all three FF days. Protein is fixed at 185 g and fat at 90 g throughout. If mid-week fatigue builds (RPE consistently >9 in metcons or lifts stall), add +20 g carbs on remaining FF days.

## Key Timing Reminders

- **Pre-LIIT fat-burner (early morning Mon/Wed/Fri):** Fasted or 500 ml water only before LIIT
- **Pre-main FF session (3–4 hours after LIIT):** 450–600 kcal with 65–80 g carbs + 35–40 g protein, fat <15 g — peak load week; fuel sessions fully
- **Post-main FF session (within 45 min):** 40–50 g protein + 65–80 g carbs, fat <10 g
- **Pre-Tuesday row (60–90 min before):** 400–500 kcal with 60–75 g carbs + 30 g protein — 6 × 800 m intervals demand consistent glycogen; do not skip pre-row fuelling
- **Pre-Sunday run (60 min before):** 300–400 kcal with 50–60 g carbs + 20–25 g protein, fat <10 g — tempo at 5:15/km requires carb priming; add 15–20 g carbs to Saturday rest-day nutrition if Sunday morning energy feels low

## Recovery & Fat-Loss Notes

- **Peak output management:** W5 stacks heavy lifts (FS 80 kg, DL 120 kg, BS 100 kg) with long, high-intensity metcons (24/25/22 min). Monitor energy daily — if Tuesday or Wednesday sessions feel flat, bump FF-day carbs to ~410 g (+20 g) for the remainder of the week.
- **HSPU tracking:** W5 targets 10+ kipping HSPU per kipping set. If shoulder fatigue carries over from heavy pressing accessory work, reduce ring push-up/dip reps rather than skipping HSPU progressions — W6 includes a max-rep HSPU test.
- **Rowing interval fuel:** W4 steady-state averaged 2:08/500 m — interval target is ≤2:05/500 m. Glycogen availability directly affects split maintenance across all 6 efforts; don't train fasted.
- **Tempo run priming:** The 6 km @ ~5:15/km is significantly harder than W4's easy run (6:40/km). If Saturday rest-day feels sluggish, add 15–20 g carbs to Saturday targets to prime Sunday glycogen stores.
- **Hydration:** 3.5–4 L water daily baseline; +500 ml per training hour. Add electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg/L) on all training days — especially Tuesday intervals and Sunday tempo.
- **W6 preview:** Week 6 is the consolidation/benchmark week with a calorie reduction (−5% on all training days, −25 g carbs). Use Saturday and Sunday of W5 to prioritize sleep and glycogen restoration heading into benchmark testing (including HSPU max-rep test).
