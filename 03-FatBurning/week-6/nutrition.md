# Week 6 Nutrition — 03-FatBurning (2026-06-15 to 2026-06-21)

## Weekly Focus

Week 6 is the **Consolidate & Test** phase. Training volume and barbell stress back off, but the week still includes meaningful outputs: Monday's Threshold retest, Friday's HSPU max-rep test, and Sunday's 5 km time trial. Apply the cycle-plan adjustment of roughly **−5% on training days** and **−25 g carbs on all training days** relative to the normal FatBurning deficit baseline. Protein stays fixed at 185 g and fat at 90 g. W5's rowing showed excellent readiness (2:03/500 m average across 6 × 800 m), while the Sunday run was skipped, so this week should prioritize honest benchmark pacing and consistent fuelling over extra fatigue.

## Key Timing Reminders

- **Pre-Monday Threshold retest:** 450–550 kcal with 60–75 g carbs + 30–35 g protein, fat <15 g, 60–90 min before the main session; 500 ml water on waking before the LIIT piece
- **Post-Monday benchmark:** 40–50 g protein + 70–80 g carbs within 45 min; keep fat minimal in that first meal
- **Pre-Tuesday 8 km row:** 400–500 kcal with 55–70 g carbs + 25–30 g protein; this row should feel smooth, not depleted
- **Pre-Friday back squat + HSPU test:** 450–550 kcal with 60–75 g carbs + 30–40 g protein; don't under-fuel just because loads are lighter
- **Pre-Sunday 5 km time trial:** 300–450 kcal with 50–65 g carbs + 20–30 g protein 60–90 min before; if the run is early morning, add 15–20 g carbs to Saturday evening intake

## Recovery & Fat-Loss Notes

- **Benchmark integrity:** Match Monday's thruster load, pull-up setup, and movement standards to the 11:30 score from Cycle 2. If assistance changes, note it and treat the result as a new reference point.
- **Do not race Tuesday's row:** W5 intervals prove the engine is there. The goal Tuesday is steady aerobic support for Sunday's run, not a second benchmark.
- **Run readiness:** Because W5's Sunday run was skipped, keep Saturday hydration high and consider the small Saturday carb bump if Sunday's time trial is in the morning.
- **Shoulder management:** Friday includes the HSPU max-rep test. If Monday's thrusters or Wednesday's dips leave the shoulders unusually cooked, reduce Wednesday/Friday accessory pressing before cutting HSPU work.
- **Adjustment rule:** If two training sessions in a row feel flat despite the lighter loads, raise remaining FF-day carbs by +20–25 g and note the response.
- **Hydration:** 3.5–4 L water daily baseline; add ~500 ml per training hour. Use electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg/L) on Monday, Friday, and Sunday in particular.
