# 03-FatBurning – Cycle Plan

## Overview

- **Focus:** Execute a sustained deficit while maintaining strength ceiling and shifting conditioning emphasis toward high-metabolic-output metcons; prioritize body composition improvement (fat loss) while preserving lean mass through consistent protein intake and moderate load retention.
- **Duration:** 6 weeks (2026-05-11 to 2026-06-19)
- **Days per week:** 5 (+ optional recovery LIIT on Thu)
  - Mon / Wed / Fri: 
    - (Fat burner) LIIT(Rope jump, row, easy running, easy METCON etc)
    - (Main) Functional fitness (strength + metcon + skill/core)
  - Tue: Indoor rowing (30–60 min, goal: 10 km under 50 min)
  - Thu: Recovery LIIT (optional: bike commute if weather is good, or 20 min light row)
  - Sat or Sun: Running (30–60 min, goal: 10 km under 50 min)

### Goals
- Maintain Front Squat, Deadlift, and Back Squat 5 RM loads within ±2 kg of Cycle 2 baselines (show strength resilience under deficit).
- Improve metcon conditioning through sustained high-output sessions (increased reps, shorter rest, metabolic focus).
- Improve rowing pace: target ≤2:28/500 m average across steady-state sessions.
- Improve running pace: sustain tempo work at ~5:15/km and maintain easy 10 km in under 50 min.
- HSPU max rep increase: test HSPU and increase max reps at the end of the cycle.

## Nutrition

### Cycle Nutrition Goals

- Maintain a **moderate caloric deficit** (~8–10% below standard baseline, matching Cycle 2 approach) across all training days while preserving 185 g protein/day and 90 g fat/day.
- **Carbohydrates are the dynamic lever:** reduce slightly on rest/easy days; maintain near-baseline on high-output FF and conditioning days to fuel metcon performance and preserve strength.
- If any athlete reports sustained fatigue (RPE consistently >9), stalled lifts, or poor sleep, raise FF-day carbs by +25–30 g for one week before reassessing.
- Monitor body composition weekly; expect ~0.5–0.75 kg/week fat loss (if loss exceeds 1 kg/week, add +100–150 kcal).

### Deficit Baseline Targets

> These replace the standard (maintenance) daily targets for this cycle. Protein (185 g) and Fat (90 g) are fixed throughout.

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Mon / Wed / Fri | ~3100 kcal | ~185 g | ~390 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance (Row / Run) | Tue / Run day (Sat or Sun) | ~2900 kcal | ~185 g | ~340 g | ~90 g |
| Recovery LIIT (Optional) | Thu | ~2400 kcal (up to +150 if needed) | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Non-training days (including one weekend day) | ~2400 kcal | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |

### Cycle Phase Adjustments

| Phase | Week | Calorie Adjustment | Carb Adjustment | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reactivation @ Deficit | Week 1 | −5% on training days from deficit baseline | −20 to −25 g on training days | Conservative first week; allow 3–4 days to adapt to deficit |
| Build Conditioning | Week 2 | At deficit baseline | At deficit baseline | Increasing metcon output — do not under-carb; match carbs to session duration/intensity |
| Push Metabolic Output | Week 3 | +3% on FF days; deficit on endurance/rest | +30 g on FF days; baseline on endurance | High-rep metcons demand fuel; protect carbs for metcon quality |
| Sustain Pace | Week 4 | −2% on FF days (slight relief); deficit baseline on endurance/rest | −15 g on FF days; baseline on endurance | Mid-cycle ease to manage fatigue; maintain deficit on lower-output days |
| Final Push | Week 5 | At deficit baseline | At deficit baseline | Last week of volume push — match carbs to training demand; some athletes may benefit from +20 g if fatigue creeps in |
| Consolidate & Test | Week 6 | −5% on all training days | −25 g on all training days | Deload week; mild deficit reduction supports recovery and confidence for benchmark attempts |

## Sample Nutrition Timeline

**Week 1–2:** Body adapts to deficit; energy may dip slightly in metcons (expected). Monitor reps and pacing; scale back if needed.

**Week 3–4:** Fat loss should be visible (~1–1.5 kg). Maintain carb balance to fuel high-output metcons.

**Week 5:** Peak output week — consider +20 g carbs on FF days if fatigue appears mid-week.

**Week 6:** Benchmark week with slightly raised carbs/calories to support testing and confidence; slight rebound in energy.
