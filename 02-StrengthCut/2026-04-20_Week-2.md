## Monday 20 April — Front Squat (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row (rate 18 spm)
  - 10 air squats with 2 s pause at bottom
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 8 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 8 hip circles/side
  - 6 world's greatest stretch/side
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 band pull-aparts

### Strength — Front Squat 4×5 (15–18 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **65 kg** (~70% of 93 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: elbows high and proud, upright torso, knees out, aggressive drive from the hole
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- **Warm-up ramp:** 35 kg × 5 → 50 kg × 3 → 60 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 10 goblet squat @ 20 kg KB — 2 s pause at bottom; chest up and knee tracking

### Metcon — AMRAP 20 min

**Format:** AMRAP 20 min. Score = total rounds + reps.

- 14 cal row — sole cardio modality
- 10 hang squat cleans @ 50 kg
- 8 chin-ups (scale: resistance band; aim unbroken through round 3)

**Target:** 4–5 rounds. Row at ≤2:32/500 m — conversational pace. Cleans should be 5+5 or better in rounds 1–3; allow 3+4+3 in later rounds. Chin-ups: break into 2 sets when needed after round 3. RPE 7–8 throughout — this is a volume session ahead of tomorrow's double.

> **Pre-fatigue note:** Tomorrow carries both deadlift and rowing. Finish today's metcon feeling like you could do one more round — do not go to failure on pull-ups.

### Skill / Core (10 min)
- **HSPU progression (4 min):**
  - 3 × 5 pike push-ups — feet elevated on bench, full ROM; rest 60 s between
  - Progress from Week 1: aim for 1 more rep on max attempt
- **Core (6 min):**
  - 3 rounds × (15 hollow rocks + 20 s hollow hold + 12 hanging knee raises)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 60 s/side
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side
- Wrist flexor & extensor stretch 45 s each
- Box breathing 4-2-6 × 2 min

## Tuesday 21 April — Deadlift + Rowing (Combined Session, ≤80 min)

> **Session structure:** Deadlift strength block first (≈35 min including warm-up), then a 5 min transition, then steady-state rowing (≈35 min including cool-down). No metcon today — the combined volume is sufficient stimulus.

### Warm-up — Deadlift (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 glute bridges + 10 bodyweight RDLs
  - 8 hip hinge with PVC (neutral spine focus) + 6 scapular push-ups
  - 8 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 6 deep reverse lunges/side

### Strength — Deadlift 4×5 (18–20 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **115 kg** (~70% of 163 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: hips back before the pull, neutral spine, drive the floor away, squeeze glutes at lockout, controlled 3 s descent on final rep of each set
  - Rest: **90 s** between sets (trimmed to keep total session time ≤80 min)
- **Warm-up ramp:** 60 kg × 5 → 85 kg × 3 → 105 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 8 single-leg RDL per side @ 16 kg KB — controlled hinge, no rush

### Transition (5 min)
- Drink 300–400 ml water + small fast carb if needed (banana, dates)
- 5 slow hip flexor rocks/side + 8 shoulder rolls — prep the rowing posture

### Rowing Warm-up (5 min)
- 3 min easy row (rate 18 spm, RPE 4)
- 10 thoracic twists seated on erg + 8 shoulder rolls
- 1 × 30 s build: 20 spm → 24 spm over the last 10 strokes

### Main Row — 7 km Steady-State
- **7,000 m at target split ≤2:33/500 m**
- Stroke rate: 20–22 spm
- **RPE:** 6–7 — fully conversational; this is aerobic base work following a strength session, not a sprint
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - First 2 km: settle in — aim 2:35–2:38. Let the deadlift fatigue flush out.
  - Middle 3 km: lock into 2:30–2:33 target zone.
  - Final 2 km: hold pace; if legs feel good, negative-split the last 1 km by 2–3 s/500 m.
  - If split drifts past 2:40 after km 3, reduce target to completing the distance at whatever even pace feels sustainable — log the split.
- **Total session time approx.:** 35–38 min row

### Rowing Cool-down (5 min)
- 2 min easy row (rate 16 spm)
- Wall calf stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Seated hip opener (figure-four on erg) 30 s/side
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 30 s/side

### Strength — Back Squat 4×5 (15–18 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **95 kg** (~71% of 134 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: big breath brace before descent, elbows up under the bar, knees track toes, drive out of the hole with intent, full depth
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- **Warm-up ramp:** 50 kg × 5 → 70 kg × 3 → 87 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 10 single-leg box step-down @ 50 cm box — 3 s controlled descent

### Metcon — 5 rounds for time (target ≤20 min)

**Format:** For time. Score = total time.

- 120 single-unders — sole cardio modality
- 10 power snatches @ 30 kg
- 15 push-ups (scale: incline push-ups on box/bench; chest to floor every rep)

**Target:** ≤20 min. Single-unders should stay relaxed and rhythmic — rest at the rope, not mid-set. Snatches at 30 kg: cycle fast — touch-and-go if form allows; catch high and punch under. Push-ups: aim for 8+7 or better early; keep the trunk rigid and avoid sagging under fatigue. RPE 7–8 through round 4, then push round 5.

> **Day-3 fatigue note:** Legs will carry some fatigue from Mon/Tue. The snatch is the primary stimulus today — if back feels heavy from yesterday's deadlift, reduce snatch to 35 kg and note it.

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 2 enters the full cut-baseline phase — the gentle Week 1 buffer is removed and daily calorie and carb targets move to the standard StrengthCut deficit. Given the compressed 3-consecutive-day training block (Mon/Tue/Wed), fuelling sequencing matters: Monday's post-session recovery feeds Tuesday's combined deadlift + row, which in turn feeds Wednesday's back squat. Do not skip the post-session protein + carb windows on Monday or Tuesday. Protein (185 g/day) is the non-negotiable anchor. During the 6-day vacation window, drop to rest-day targets — the body will use this time for recovery, and over-eating on holiday is not needed. Re-establish training-day targets from Wednesday 29 April.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Mon 20 / Tue 21 / Wed 22 | ~3100 kcal | ~185 g | ~390 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Thu 23 – Wed 28 (vacation) | ~2400 kcal | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |

> Vacation days: if you do an optional run or swim, add ~30–40 g carbs (and ~200 kcal) on that day to support the effort. Otherwise hold rest-day targets.

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Front Squat (Monday):** 400–500 kcal, 55–65 g carbs, 30–35 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water.
- **Post-Monday (critical):** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g fast carbs within 45 min — tomorrow carries both deadlift and a 7 km row; glycogen must be ready overnight.
- **Pre-Deadlift + Row (Tuesday):** 500–600 kcal, 70–80 g carbs, 35–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 90 min before; 500 ml water + electrolytes (500–700 mg sodium). The combined session demands sustained energy.
- **Post-Row (Tuesday):** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g carbs within 45 min; 750 ml water + electrolytes. Back squat is tomorrow — replace glycogen fully tonight.
- **Pre-Back Squat (Wednesday):** 400–500 kcal, 55–65 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Tuesday night's recovery meal does the heavy lifting here.

### Recovery Notes
- **Mon → Tue → Wed back-to-back-to-back:** Three consecutive training days in a cut is demanding. Sleep 8 h each night, hydrate to 3.5–4 L + 500 ml/hr training; include sodium and potassium on the rowing day. If Wednesday's back squat warm-up ramp feels heavy, drop working sets to 90 kg (no lower) and note it — loading will re-assess in Week 3.
- **Vacation:** This break arrives at the perfect time in the cycle. Digest the training stimulus, sleep well, eat at rest-day targets. Returning well-rested to Week 3′s Push phase is more valuable than any extra sessions during the break.
- **Performance check:** If Tuesday's combined session produces any unusual lumbar or hip tightness, flag it before Week 3 and adjust deadlift load accordingly.
