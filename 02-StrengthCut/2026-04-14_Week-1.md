## Monday — Front Squat (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row (rate 18 spm)
  - 10 air squats with 2 s pause at bottom
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 8 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 8 hip circles/side
  - 6 world's greatest stretch/side (lunge + thoracic rotation)
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 band pull-aparts

### Strength — Front Squat 4×5 (15–18 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **57.5 kg** (~62% of 93 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: elbows high and proud, upright torso, knees out, full depth, aggressive drive from the hole
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- **Warm-up ramp:** 30 kg × 5 → 40 kg × 5 → 50 kg × 3
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 10 goblet squat @ 16 kg KB — 2 s pause at bottom; focus on chest up and knee tracking

### Metcon — AMRAP 16 min

**Format:** AMRAP 16 min. Score = total rounds + reps.

- 12 cal row — sole cardio modality
- 16 KB swings @ 20 kg (American — overhead)
- 8 strict pull-ups (scale: resistance band to maintain full range; aim to complete each set unbroken in rounds 1–3)

**Target:** 4–5 rounds. Keep the row at a conversational pace (≤2:35/500 m) — this is a volume session, not a sprint. KB swings unbroken throughout. Pull-ups can break to 2 sets in later rounds.

> **Note:** This reactivation metcon is intentionally light. If you finish over 5 rounds cleanly, note it — load goes up next week.

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- **HSPU progression (5 min):**
  - 3 × 5 pike push-ups — feet elevated on bench, full ROM (head to floor → lock out); rest 60 s between
  - 1 × max-rep attempt — note reps
- **DU practice (4 min):**
  - 4 sets of 30 s work: singles → attempt 5–10 consecutive DU → back to singles. Focus on wrist mechanics and timing, not volume.
- **Core (3 min):**
  - 3 rounds × (15 hollow rocks + 20 s hollow hold)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 60 s/side (front squat hip flexor demand)
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side
- Wrist flexor & extensor stretch 45 s each (front rack demand)
- Box breathing 4-2-6 × 2 min

## Tuesday — Indoor Rowing (Carry-over from Cycle 1 Week 4)

*This session was originally scheduled for Cycle 1, Week 4 Tuesday. It was not executed. Content is identical to the original Week 3 prescription.*

### Warm-up (8 min)
- 3 min easy row (rate 18 spm, long slow stroke, relaxed — RPE 4)
- 10 thoracic twists seated on erg + 10 shoulder rolls + 8 hip flexor rocks/side
- 2 × 30 s build: start at 22 spm → push to 26 spm in the last 5 strokes; easy 30 s between

### Main Row — 5 × 1,500 m Intervals (VO₂max target)
- **5 rounds of:**
  - **1,500 m at target split ≤2:25/500 m (RPE 8–9)**
  - Stroke rate: 24–26 spm
  - Rest: **3 min easy paddle** between intervals (rate 16–18 spm — do NOT stop; keep moving)
- **Total work:** 7,500 m | **Total session time approx.:** 45–50 min
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Hold back — aim for 2:28–2:30. The first interval almost always feels deceptively easy.
  - Intervals 2–4: Settle into 2:24–2:26, your target zone.
  - Interval 5: Race — whatever you have left, push; target ≤2:22.
  - If any interval slips past 2:35, end the session there, note it, and recalibrate.

### Alternative Main Row — 6 × 500 m Sprints (use if fatigued from Monday or short on time)

- **6 rounds of:**
  - **500 m at target split ≤2:10/500 m (RPE 9)**
  - Stroke rate: 26–28 spm
  - Rest: **90 s complete rest** between intervals
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Conservative — aim for 2:12–2:15.
  - Intervals 2–5: Lock into ≤2:10.
  - Interval 6: All-out — target ≤2:05.

### Cool-down (5 min)
- 2 min easy row (rate 16 spm)
- Wall calf stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Seated hip opener (figure-four on erg) 30 s/side
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 30 s/side

## Wednesday — Deadlift (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 glute bridges + 10 bodyweight RDLs
  - 8 hip hinge with PVC (neutral spine focus) + 6 scapular push-ups
  - 8 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 6 deep reverse lunges/side
  - 8 KB Romanian deadlifts @ 16 kg — feel the hamstring load

### Strength — Deadlift 4×5 (15–18 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **100 kg** (~61% of 163 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: hips back before the pull, neutral spine throughout, drive the floor away, squeeze glutes at lockout, controlled 3 s descent on final rep of each set
  - Rest: 2 min between sets
- **Warm-up ramp:** 60 kg × 5 → 80 kg × 3 → 92 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 8 single-leg RDL per side @ 16 kg KB — control the hip hinge; don't rush

### Metcon — 5 rounds for time (target ≤17 min)

**Format:** For time. Score = total time.

- 30 double-unders (alt: 90 single-unders) — sole cardio modality
- 9 hang power cleans @ 55 kg
- 7 ring dips (scale: reduce to 5 reps or feet-assisted to maintain full ROM)

**Target:** ≤17 min. DU set is short enough to complete in one pass in early rounds — if breaking, do SU singles between attempts rather than resting. Hang power cleans at 55 kg should be 5+4 or 4+3+2 in early rounds. Ring dips: control the descent every rep; no collapsing at the bottom.

> **Loading note:** 55 kg is intentionally lower than you're capable of — this is a first-week pacing and volume check. Note how the cleans feel and what loads you'd want for Week 2 and 3.

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- **HSPU progression (5 min):**
  - 3 × 3 wall walks — walk hands in as close to the wall as possible; hold nose-to-wall for 5 s at the top; walk back down controlled; rest 60 s between
  - Goal: each walk gets hands ~5 cm closer to the wall than last week
- **DU practice (4 min):**
  - 4 × 30 s: attempt consecutive DU — count your longest unbroken set, note it
- **Core (3 min):**
  - 3 rounds × (12 dead bugs/side + 20 s side plank/side)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Prone quad stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Lat band stretch 2 × 45 s/side (ring dip lat demand)
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 2 × 45 s/side (deadlift + swing hip drive)
- Supine 90/90 diaphragmatic breathing × 2 min

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 1 of the StrengthCut cycle — the cut starts here. Calories and carbs are set at 5% below the cut baseline on all training days, giving the body time to adjust without immediately compromising performance under new loads. The primary goal this week is to establish the deficit gently: eating consistently at these targets (not lower) is more important than perfect adherence to timing. Protein must hit 185 g/day — it is the muscle-preservation anchor during any cut. The Tuesday rowing session is the hardest effort of the week despite being a carry-over; fuel it properly with a pre-session meal and replace glycogen immediately after. If energy or mood drops noticeably by Thursday, add 30 g carbs to Friday's pre-session window and track the effect.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Mon / Wed / Fri | ~2950 kcal | ~185 g | ~350 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance (Row / Run) | Tue / Thu | ~2750 kcal | ~185 g | ~300 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Sat / Sun | ~2400 kcal | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Front Squat (Monday):** 400–500 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 30–35 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water.
- **Post-Monday / Pre-Tuesday row:** Prioritise 40–50 g protein + 50–60 g fast carbs within 45 min of finishing Monday's session. The Tuesday VO₂max row demands pre-loaded glycogen — do not skip this window.
- **Pre-Row Intervals (Tuesday):** 300–400 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 25–30 g protein — 60 min before; 500 ml water + electrolytes (sodium 500–700 mg). Five 1,500 m efforts at RPE 8–9 will spike sweat rate.
- **Post-Row (Tuesday):** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g carbs within 45 min; 750 ml water + electrolytes. Deadlift session tomorrow — glycogen replacement tonight matters.
- **Pre-Deadlift (Wednesday):** 400–500 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Tuesday night nutrition is the fuelling window here.
- **Pre-Run (Thursday):** 300 kcal, 40–50 g carbs — 60 min before; 500 ml water. Easy zone-2 run — no need to overload; keep it light and digestible.
- **Pre-Back Squat (Friday):** 400–500 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water.

### Recovery Notes
- **Tue–Wed back-to-back (hard row + deadlift):** Tuesday night sleep and nutrition are non-negotiable. Aim for 8 h sleep. The row will deplete glycogen significantly — replace it in full before Wednesday.
- **Daily hydration baseline:** 3.5–4 L water; add ~500 ml per hour of training. Include sodium (500–700 mg/L) and potassium on Tuesday (rowing intervals) and Thursday (running).
- **Weekend:** Sat/Sun are genuine recovery — walk, stretch, sleep 8+ h. Do not add any extra training sessions to compensate for the cut.
- **Gut-check:** If two or more sessions this week feel flat or lifts feel uncharacteristically heavy, raise FF-day carbs to the full cut-baseline level (~390 g) from Week 2 onwards before pulling any other lever.
