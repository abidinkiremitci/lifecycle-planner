# Week 4 – Consolidate + Benchmark Retest (2026-05-05 to 2026-05-10)

## Weekly Overview
- Cycle: 02-StrengthCut
- Week: 4
- Dates: 2026-05-05 to 2026-05-10
- Load target: ~70–75%
- Metcon target: 20–25 min (benchmark retest week)
- Focus: Consolidate after the push week, keep the barbell work crisp at moderate loads, and retest all three cycle benchmarks while finishing the endurance block with an 8 km steady row and a 5 km run time trial.
- Days: Tuesday 5 May (FF – Front Squat + Threshold retest), Wednesday 6 May (Row), Thursday 7 May (Run time trial), Friday 8 May (FF – Deadlift + Engine retest), Saturday 9 May (FF – Back Squat + Finisher retest)

## Notes
- **Retest week:** This week is about clean comparisons, not accumulating extra fatigue. Strength is deliberately backed off to ~70–75% so the benchmark pieces are the priority.
- **Benchmarks to retest:**
  - **Threshold:** 21-15-9 Thrusters 40 kg / Strict Pull-ups — previous 13:05
  - **Engine:** 3 rounds of 12 cal row / 21 KB swings 24 kg / 12 ring rows — previous 10:30
  - **Finisher:** 3 rounds of 15 box step-ups / 10 hang squat cleans 50 kg / 8 ring dips — previous 9:32
- **Pacing rule:** Do not PR the first 3 minutes of any retest. Start controlled, then build. A valid apples-to-apples score matters more than a reckless opening sprint.
- **After Week 3:** If Thursday's rowing intervals or Friday's heavy metcon left any lingering posterior-chain tightness, trim Tuesday's front squat load by 2.5–5 kg and preserve the benchmark instead.
- One cardio movement per metcon only.
- No kipping pull-ups, toes-to-bar, or chest-to-bar.
- No bench press, strict press, or back squats in metcons.
- Space: 5 m × 4 m — short shuttles only, no continuous carries > 20 m.
- **Cutting cycle:** Stay at cut baseline this week. If benchmark performance tanks across two separate sessions, raise FF-day carbs by +30–40 g immediately rather than forcing the deficit harder.
- **Sunday 10 May:** Full rest, easy walk, mobility, and cycle review only.

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 4 is the Consolidate phase and the final week of the cycle. Calories and carbs return to the standard cut baseline after the Week 3 push bump, but this is not the week to under-fuel: benchmark retests and the 5 km time trial need stable glycogen to produce meaningful results. Protein remains fixed at 185 g/day. The priority is consistency: hit pre-session carbs, recover hard after the retests, and keep hydration high so the scores reflect actual fitness rather than under-fuelling. If two benchmark sessions feel flat or drop clearly below expectation, add +30–40 g carbs on FF days immediately and note the response.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Tue 5 / Fri 8 / Sat 9 | ~3100 kcal | ~185 g | ~390 g | ~90 g |
| Endurance (Row / Run) | Wed 6 / Thu 7 | ~2900 kcal | ~185 g | ~340 g | ~90 g |
| Rest / Active Recovery | Sun 10 | ~2400 kcal | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Threshold retest (Tuesday):** 450–550 kcal, 60–70 g carbs, 30–35 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. This benchmark punishes low glycogen immediately.
- **Post-Tuesday:** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g carbs within 45 min. Wednesday's 8 km row is not maximal, but it still needs topped-up glycogen.
- **Pre-5 km time trial (Thursday):** 350–450 kcal, 50–60 g carbs, 25–30 g protein, <10 g fat — 60 min before; 500 ml water. Keep it light and digestible.
- **Post-time trial (Thursday):** 40–50 g protein + 50–60 g carbs within 45 min; 500–750 ml water. Friday's Engine retest comes fast.
- **Pre-Engine retest (Friday):** 400–500 kcal, 55–65 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water.
- **Pre-Finisher retest (Saturday):** 400–500 kcal, 55–65 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Friday night recovery matters here.

### Recovery Notes
- **Tue / Wed / Thu / Fri / Sat run:** Five consecutive training days, even with moderate strength loading, is still a dense finish to the cycle. Sleep 8 h per night, hydrate at 3.5–4 L baseline plus ~500 ml/hr training, and include sodium/potassium on rowing and running days.
- **Retest integrity:** Keep loading, ring setup, box height, and movement standards as close to Cycle 1 as possible. A slower but honest benchmark is more useful than a faster one with changed standards.
- **Sunday:** No make-up work. Walk, stretch, review scores, and let the cycle close properly.
- **Adjustment rule:** If two lifts in a row feel flat or benchmark power output drops sharply, raise FF-day carbs by +30–40 g immediately and reassess before changing volume or movement selection.
