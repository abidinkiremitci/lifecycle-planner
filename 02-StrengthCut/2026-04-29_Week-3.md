# Week 3 – Push (2026-04-29 to 2026-05-03)

## Weekly Overview
- Cycle: 02-StrengthCut
- Week: 3
- Dates: 2026-04-29 to 2026-05-03
- Load target: ~75–80%
- Metcon target: 20–25 min
- Focus: Return from vacation with the heaviest loads of the cycle; push the strength ceiling on all three lifts; execute the 4 × 2,000 m rowing session combined with deadlift Thursday; close the week with a 6 km tempo run Saturday and an optional easy run Sunday.
- Days: Wednesday 29 Apr (FF – Front Squat), Thursday 30 Apr (FF – Deadlift + Rowing), Friday 1 May (FF – Back Squat), Saturday 2 May (Running – Main), Sunday 3 May (Running – Optional easy)

## Notes
- **Return from vacation:** Week 3 is the Push phase — but first session back (Wednesday) should feel out the legs before committing. If the warm-up ramp feels heavy, drop the working sets by 5 kg and note it. Trust the cycle structure.
- **Consecutive Thu/Fri sessions:** Deadlift + Row Thursday, Back Squat Friday. Both are demanding. Thursday night nutrition and sleep are essential — treat them as pre-match fuelling.
- **Load targets:** Front Squat ~72 kg / Deadlift ~125 kg / Back Squat ~103 kg (~77% est. 1RM across the board).
- **Running:** Saturday is the main run (6 km tempo). Sunday is an optional easy Zone 2 run only if legs feel fine — skip it if there's any tightness in the posterior chain after Thursday's deadlift.
- One cardio movement per metcon only.
- No kipping pull-ups, toes-to-bar, or chest-to-bar.
- No bench press, strict press, or back squats in metcons.
- Space: 5 m × 4 m — short shuttles only, no continuous carries > 20 m.
- **Cutting cycle:** FF-day carbs step up +35 g vs. cut baseline this week to support the heavier loading. If two sessions feel flat, add another +30 g and note the response.

## Wednesday 29 April — Front Squat (Functional Fitness)

### Warm-up (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row (rate 18 spm)
  - 10 air squats with 3 s pause at bottom (extra time to feel the bottom position after vacation)
  - 8 scapular pull-ups + 8 PVC pass-throughs
  - 8 glute bridges + 8 hip circles/side
  - 6 world's greatest stretch/side
  - 10 shoulder rolls + 5 band pull-aparts

### Strength — Front Squat 4×5 (15–18 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **72 kg** (~77% of 93 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: elbows high and proud, upright torso, knees out, aggressive drive from the hole — these reps should feel heavier than Week 2; expect each set to take longer
  - Rest: 2.5 min between sets (extra rest for the heavier load)
- **Warm-up ramp:** 40 kg × 5 → 55 kg × 3 → 65 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 3 × 8 goblet squat @ 20 kg KB — 2 s pause at bottom; prioritise knee tracking and chest position

### Metcon — AMRAP 22 min

**Format:** AMRAP 22 min. Score = total rounds + reps.

- 16 cal row — sole cardio modality
- 12 thrusters @ 40 kg
- 8 strict pull-ups (scale: band-assisted; every rep full ROM — chin clearly over bar)

**Target:** 4–5 rounds. Row at ≤2:30/500 m — this week the row pace tightens by 2–3 s vs. Week 2. Thrusters at 40 kg: squat clean the first rep; cycle fast through sets of 6+6 or 4+4+4 in later rounds. Pull-ups: break into 2 sets if needed after round 3. RPE 8 from round 2 onwards.

> **Post-vacation check:** If round 1 feels significantly harder than expected, check your pacing — the vacation may have softened the aerobic engine slightly. Adjust row to ≤2:35 and hold; do not blow up round 1.

### Skill / Core (12 min)
- **HSPU progression (5 min):**

  - 1 × max-effort freestanding kick-up attempt — hold any balance you find; log duration
- **DU practice (4 min):**
  - 5 × 20 s: attempt consecutive DU — focus on rhythm; log longest unbroken set
- **Core (3 min):**
  - 3 rounds × (15 hollow rocks + 15 weighted sit-ups @ 10 kg plate)

### Cool-down (10 min)
- Couch stretch 2 × 60 s/side
- Seated single-leg hamstring fold 2 × 45 s/side
- Wall lat stretch palm-up 2 × 45 s/side
- Wrist flexor & extensor stretch 45 s each

## Thursday 30 April — Deadlift + Rowing (Combined Session, ≤85 min)

> **Session structure:** Deadlift strength block first (≈38 min including warm-up and accessory), 5 min transition, then 4 × 2,000 m rowing intervals (≈40 min). No metcon today.

### Warm-up — Deadlift (10 min)
- 2 rounds:
  - 60 s easy row
  - 10 glute bridges + 10 bodyweight RDLs
  - 8 hip hinge with PVC (neutral spine) + 6 scapular push-ups
  - 8 leg swings/side (forward + lateral) + 6 deep reverse lunges/side
  - 8 KB Romanian deadlifts @ 20 kg — hamstring load primer

### Strength — Deadlift 4×5 (18–20 min)
- **Working sets:** 4 × 5 @ **125 kg** (~77% of 163 kg est. 1RM)
  - Cues: hips back and high, neutral spine, drive the floor away, exhale at lockout, controlled 3 s descent on final rep of each set — do NOT rush the eccentric at this load
  - Rest: 2.5 min between sets
- **Warm-up ramp:** 60 kg × 5 → 90 kg × 3 → 112 kg × 2
- **Accessory:**
  - 2 × 8 single-leg RDL per side @ 20 kg KB — slow and controlled; this is hamstring-tendon prep for the row

### Transition (5 min)
- 300–400 ml water + fast carb if needed
- 5 slow hip flexor rocks/side + 8 shoulder rolls + 5 thoracic twists

### Rowing Warm-up (5 min)
- 3 min easy row (rate 18 spm, RPE 4)
- 10 thoracic twists on erg + 8 shoulder rolls + 8 hip flexor rocks/side
- 2 × 30 s build: 20 spm → 26 spm in the final 5 strokes; 30 s easy between

### Main Row — 4 × 2,000 m Intervals
- **4 rounds of:**
  - **2,000 m at target split ≤2:30/500 m (RPE 8–9)**
  - Stroke rate: 24–26 spm
  - Rest: **3 min easy paddle** between intervals (rate 16–18 spm — keep moving, do not stop)
- **Total work:** 8,000 m | **Total session time approx.:** 38–42 min
- **Pacing guidance:**
  - Interval 1: Hold back — aim for 2:32–2:34. Deadlift fatigue will make the first 500 m feel heavier than expected.
  - Intervals 2–3: Settle into 2:28–2:30 target zone.
  - Interval 4: Race it — whatever you have left; target ≤2:25.
  - If any interval slips past 2:40, drop to 3 total intervals and note it — the dead + row combination at this load is a first test.

### Rowing Cool-down (5 min)
- 2 min easy row (rate 16 spm)
- Wall calf stretch 2 × 45 s/side
- Seated hip opener (figure-four on erg) 30 s/side
- Kneeling hip flexor lunge stretch 30 s/side

## Nutrition

### Weekly Focus
Week 3 is the Push phase — the heaviest loads of the cycle land this week, and the matched FF-day carb targets step up by +35 g above cut baseline to protect lifting output while maintaining the overall deficit. The Deadlift + Row double on Thursday is the most demanding session of the week: fuelling it front and back is essential. Protein stays fixed at 185 g/day — in a push week under a caloric deficit, protein is the primary defence against muscle catabolism. Saturday's tempo run is the first real running intensity session of the cycle; treat the pre-run meal seriously despite it being a weekend. If any two sessions this week feel flat or lifts regress vs. targets, raise all FF-day carbs to 450 g immediately (without waiting for Week 4) and log the response.

### Macro Targets This Week

| Day Type | Days | Calories | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Fitness | Wed 29 / Thu 30 / Fri 1 | ~3250 kcal | ~185 g | ~425 g | ~90 g |
| Running (Main) | Sat 2 May | ~2900 kcal | ~185 g | ~340 g | ~90 g |
| Running (Optional Easy) / Rest | Sun 3 May | ~2400 kcal | ~185 g | ~210 g | ~90 g |

> If Sunday run is skipped, hold rest-day targets (~2400 kcal / 210 g carbs). If it is completed, step up to ~2600 kcal / ~260 g carbs.

### Key Timing Reminders
- **Pre-Front Squat (Wednesday):** 500–600 kcal, 65–75 g carbs, 35–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. This is the heaviest front squat of the cycle.
- **Post-Wednesday (critical):** 40–50 g protein + 60–70 g fast carbs within 45 min. Tomorrow carries both deadlift and 4 × 2,000 m intervals — glycogen replacement overnight is essential.
- **Pre-Deadlift + Row (Thursday):** 500–600 kcal, 70–80 g carbs, 35–40 g protein, <15 g fat — 90 min before; 500 ml water + electrolytes (500–700 mg sodium + potassium). The rowing intervals at RPE 8–9 post-deadlift will spike sweat rate significantly.
- **Intra-row (Thursday):** Keep 1–2 dates or a sports drink on hand. 8,000 m of interval work plus a deadlift session will demand carb support — sip between intervals if needed.
- **Post-Row (Thursday):** 40–50 g protein + 70–80 g carbs within 45 min; 750 ml water + electrolytes. Back squat is tomorrow at 103 kg — this recovery window is non-negotiable.
- **Pre-Back Squat (Friday):** 400–500 kcal, 60–70 g carbs, 30–35 g protein — 60–90 min before; 500 ml water. Thursday night nutrition and 8 h sleep do the heavy lifting here.
- **Pre-Tempo Run (Saturday):** 400–500 kcal, 55–65 g carbs, 25–30 g protein, <15 g fat — 60 min before; 500 ml water. Keep fat low for fast gastric emptying.
- **Post-Tempo Run (Saturday):** 40–50 g protein + 50–60 g carbs within 45 min; 500 ml water.

### Recovery Notes
- **Wed → Thu → Fri back-to-back-to-back at 77%:** The three consecutive heavy sessions are the hardest block of the cycle. Sleep is the primary recovery tool — aim for 8 h each night. Hydration baseline 3.5–4 L + 500 ml/hr training; sodium and potassium mandatory on Thursday (rowing intervals).
- **Thu posterior-chain carry-over into Fri:** If lower back or hamstrings feel tight Friday morning after Thursday's combined session, tack 2 min of slow hip hinge PVC work onto the warm-up and drop back squat working sets by 5 kg (98 kg). Log the adjustment — it does not count as a failure; it's data.
- **Weekend running:** Saturday tempo is the priority. Sunday easy run is optional — the right call is to skip it if there is any doubt. Week 4 retests begin Monday and you want to arrive fresh.
- **Performance check:** Week 4 is benchmark week. Everything done this week is a final stimulus before the retest. If lifts hit their targets cleanly and rowing intervals hold ≤2:30, Week 4 positioning is strong. Log everything accurately.
